# Cao Cao's Conversations and Recorded Speeches (Research)

This document explores the verbal interactions, speeches, and behavioral patterns of Cao Cao, focusing on his pragmatic communication style, his relationship with advisors, and his use of improvisational analogies.

## 1. Core Dialogues with Advisors

### 1.1 Xun Yu (荀彧): The Strategic Conscience
*   **The "Ten Victories and Ten Defeats" (十胜十败论):** While often attributed to Guo Jia, Xun Yu provided a similar strategic framework (the "Four Victories") before the Battle of Guandu. Cao Cao’s reaction was one of high morale and immediate alignment: "If I cannot achieve this, how can I face the world?"
*   **The "Duke of Wei" Conflict (进爵魏公之争):**
    *   **The Incident:** When Cao Cao sought the title of Duke of Wei and the Nine Bestowments (九锡), Xun Yu opposed it, arguing it betrayed the original intent of restoring the Han.
    *   **Reaction to Questioning:** *Sanguozhi* records that Cao Cao's "heart was not at peace" (心不能平). This shows a cold, internal withdrawal rather than an immediate outburst.
    *   **The Symbolic Conclusion:** The story of the **Empty Food Box** (from Pei Songzhi's annotations and *Shishuo Xinyu*) serves as a silent, lethal dialogue. Sending an empty box signaled: "There is no more for you to consume" (no more rank/food) or "We have nothing left to say."

### 1.2 Guo Jia (郭嘉): The Tactical Mirror
*   **"Military Operations Favor Speed" (兵贵神速):** During the northern expedition against the Wuhuan, Cao Cao was hesitant due to the harsh conditions. Guo Jia’s advice to "abandon the baggage and strike with light cavalry" resonated with Cao Cao's own improvisational nature.
*   **Liao Dong Strategy:** After Guo Jia’s death, Cao Cao followed his "posthumous strategy" to wait for the Gongsun brothers to turn on the Yuan brothers. Cao Cao’s ability to "wait out" an enemy based on an advisor's psychological profile shows a high degree of trust and strategic patience.

### 1.3 Jia Xu (贾诩): The Humanist Realist
*   **The Succession Crisis:** When Cao Cao asked Jia Xu about choosing between Cao Pi and Cao Zhi, Jia Xu remained silent. When pressed, he said, "I was thinking of Yuan Shao and Liu Biao."
    *   **Reaction to Analogies:** Cao Cao laughed immediately (大笑). He recognized the historical precedent (destruction caused by choosing a younger son) and changed his stance, confirming Cao Pi as the heir.
*   **The "Muffled Letter" (抹书间韩遂):** Jia Xu suggested sending a letter with heavy redactions to Han Sui to make Ma Chao suspicious. Cao Cao executed this with precision, showing his love for "tricky" (假谲) but effective psychological tactics.

## 2. Speeches and Public Remarks

### 2.1 Pre-Battle Oratory
*   **Battle of Guandu:** Confronted with Yuan Shao's overwhelming numbers, Cao Cao addressed his commanders not with false hope, but with a cold analysis of Yuan Shao’s character flaws (indecisive, broad but lacking focus). He used himself as a benchmark: "I know my own wisdom and my soldiers' courage; we can overcome them."
*   **Rewarding the Dead:** Cao Cao’s speeches regarding fallen soldiers often focused on the "Great Order" (大纲) rather than personal glory. He frequently used his poems (like *Hao Li Xing*) as public laments to build a shared sense of tragic duty with his men.

### 2.2 Political Self-Justification
*   **"Rang Xian Zi Shu" (让县自明本志令):** His famous open letter/speech defending his power.
    *   **Key Phrase:** "If the state had not me, I know not how many would call themselves Emperor, and how many would call themselves King."
    *   **Tone:** Assertive, pragmatic, and dismissive of "empty reputation" (虚名).

## 3. Informal Remarks and Improvisational Analogies

### 3.1 The "Wang Mei" (望梅止渴) Logic
*   **The Analogy:** "Ahead is a large forest of plums, the fruit is sweet and sour, it can quench your thirst."
*   **The Focus:** This was not a lie of convenience, but an **improvisational sensory manipulation**. Cao Cao understood that the *idea* of sourness would trigger a physical reaction (salivation), buying time for the army.

### 3.2 The "Chicken Ribs" (鸡肋) Incident
*   **The Analogy:** During the Hanzhong campaign, the night password was "Chicken Ribs."
*   **The Logic:** "Tasteless to eat, but a waste to throw away." It perfectly captured a strategic stalemate. While he executed Yang Xiu for revealing the secret, the analogy itself remains a pinnacle of his "minimalist" descriptive style.

### 3.3 Intellectual Competition (Shishuo Xinyu - 捷悟篇)
*   **"Kuo" (阔) on the Gate:** Cao Cao wrote the character "Live" (活) inside the character for "Door" (门) to signify the gate was too wide (阔).
*   **"One-Box-Sux" (一合酥):** He wrote "He" (合) on a box of snacks, meaning "One person, one mouthful" (一人一口).
*   **Reaction to Equals:** When Yang Xiu deciphered the "Cao E Monument" riddle thirty miles before Cao Cao did, he remarked: "My talent does not reach yours by thirty miles" (我才不及卿，乃觉三十里). This shows a rare, albeit competitive, admission of another's wit.

## 4. Summary of Behavioral Patterns

| Behavior | Pattern in Dialogue |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Reaction to Criticism** | Cold withdrawal or "heart not at peace" (with peers); immediate laughter/adoption if the logic is historical/pragmatic. |
| **Improvisational Analogies** | Uses sensory or mundane objects (Plums, Chicken ribs, Dragons, Sux) to explain complex strategic or psychological states. |
| **Moments of Changing Stance** | Usually triggered by an advisor using a historical negative example (e.g., Jia Xu's "Yuan Shao/Liu Biao" remark). |
| **Speech Style** | Extremely concise, avoids "empty words," focuses on the "real harm" (实祸) versus "empty name" (虚名). |

*Sources: Sanguozhi (Chen Shou), Pei Songzhi's Annotations (citing Wei Shi Chun Qiu, Fu Zi), Shishuo Xinyu (chapters on Wisdom, Intelligence, and Falsehood).*
