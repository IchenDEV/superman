# Galileo Galilei · 01-Writings.md

## Core Intellectual Frameworks

### 1. Mathematical Realism (The Book of Nature)
- **Source:** *The Assayer* (1623), *Dialogo* (1632).
- **Core Argument:** The universe is not a qualitative mystery but a structured system governed by mathematical laws. "Philosophy is written in this grand book... it is written in the language of mathematics, and its characters are triangles, circles, and other geometric figures." [Ref: *The Assayer*]
- **Implication:** Sensory experience must be translated into quantitative measurement to be understood.

### 2. Sensata Esperienza (The Empirical Loop)
- **Source:** *Discorsi* (1638).
- **Core Argument:** Knowledge comes from a cycle of **Intuition** (isolating variables), **Demonstration** (mathematical proof), and **Experiment** (physical verification). [Ref: umces.edu]
- **Pedigree:** Rejection of Aristotle's *ipse dixit* (he said it) in favor of direct observation through tools like the telescope and the inclined plane.

### 3. Galilean Relativity
- **Source:** *Dialogo sopra i due massimi sistemi del mondo* (1632).
- **Core Argument:** Inertial invariance. Using the "Ship Thought Experiment" to explain why we don't feel the Earth's motion. If you are in the hold of a ship, no experiment can tell you if the ship is moving smoothly or at rest. [Ref: *Dialogo*, Second Day]

### 4. Controlled Experiment & Abstraction
- **Source:** *Discorsi e dimostrazioni matematiche intorno a due nuove scienze* (1638).
- **Core Argument:** The "fateful omission." To find the pure law of motion (parabolic paths, constant acceleration), one must ignore "impediments" like friction or air resistance in the mathematical model, then add them back as corrections. [Ref: *Discorsi*]

## Self-Created Terms & Concepts
- **"Medicean Stars"**: The moons of Jupiter (strategic naming for patronage).
- **"New Sciences"**: Kinematics and Strength of Materials.
- **"Simplicio"**: The persona used to represent (and mock) the stubborn Aristotelian view.

## Intellectual Pedigree
- **Ancestors**: Archimedes (mathematical physics), Euclid (geometry), Copernicus (heliocentrism).
- **Descendants**: Newton (formalization of motion), Einstein (relativity), Hawking. [Ref: britannica.com]
