# 02-conversations.md - Satoshi Nakamoto's Interactive Thinking

## Communication Channels
- **Cryptography Mailing List**: Early technical debates.
- **Bitcointalk Forums**: Community building and troubleshooting.
- **Private Emails**: Mike Hearn, Gavin Andresen, Hal Finney.

## Key Interactions
- **The "Sorry" Response (to Dan Larimer)**: "If you don't believe me or don't get it, I don't have time to try to convince you, sorry." (July 29, 2010). Shows technical impatience with those who miss fundamental concepts.
- **Handover to Gavin Andresen (April 2011)**: "I wish you wouldn't keep talking about me as a mysterious shadowy figure... Maybe instead make it about the open source project."
- **Handover to Mike Hearn (April 23, 2011)**: "I've moved on to other things. It's in good hands with Gavin and everyone."
- **WikiLeaks Controversy**: Warned against WikiLeaks using Bitcoin too early: "The project needs to grow gradually so the software can be hardened along the way. I make this appeal to WikiLeaks not to try to use Bitcoin... Bitcoin is a small beta community in its infancy."

## Behavioral Observations
- **Technical Patience vs. Social Impatience**: Patient in debugging (Hal Finney emails), but impatient with social drama or "myth-making."
- **Collective Framing**: Almost always used "we" when describing the technical proposal, even if he worked alone initially.
- **Pseudonymous Detachment**: Never disclosed personal details. Focused entirely on the code and the protocol.

## Information Reliability
- **Trustworthiness**: 5/5 (Direct primary source emails and posts).
- **Type**: Interactive, reactive.
