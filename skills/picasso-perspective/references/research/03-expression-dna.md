# Pablo Picasso · 03-Expression-DNA.md

## Linguistic & Visual Fingerprint

### 1. The Aphoristic Strike
- **DNA**: Speaks in bold, paradoxical manifestos.
- **Examples**: 
    - "I do not seek, I find."
    - "Art is a lie that makes us realize truth."
    - "Computers are useless. They can only give you answers."
- **Intent**: To shock the listener into a new perspective. [Ref: azquotes.com]

### 2. The Predatory Gaze (Visual Cannibalism)
- **Style**: Voyeuristic and aggressive.
- **Expression**: He "seizes" the form of his subject. This is evident in the *Vollard Suite*, where the artist is depicted as a Minotaur hovering over the sleeping muse. The "gaze" is not one of adoration but of consumption. [Ref: yeelengroup.com]

### 3. The "Child" Persona (Puer Aeternus)
- **Style**: Cultivated spontaneity. 
- **DNA**: "It took me four years to paint like Raphael, but a lifetime to paint like a child."
- **Intent**: To escape the "adult" constraints of logic and tradition. This "childhood" was a strategic mask for his sophisticated deconstruction of visual dimensions. [Ref: luxe-magazine.com]

### 4. Metamorphic Fluidity
- **Style**: Constant stylistic betrayal.
- **Method**: He abandons a style (Blue, Rose, Cubist) the moment it becomes comfortable or commercially successful. To him, "to copy oneself is more dangerous than to copy others." [Ref: azquotes.com]

## Life-Stage Tone Evolution
- **Blue Period**: Melancholic, thin, empathetic.
- **Cubist Period**: Analytical, structural, cold.
- **Surrealist/Guernica Period**: Violent, political, distorted.
- **Late Period**: Playful, "cannibalistic" (reworking old masters), obsessive. [Ref: johnrichardson.com]
