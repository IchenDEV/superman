# Galileo Galilei · 06-Timeline.md

| Year | Milestone | Impact on Thinking |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1564 | Born in Pisa | Early exposure to music and mathematics (son of Vincenzo Galilei). |
| 1581 | University of Pisa | Begins as a medical student but switches to mathematics after observing a swinging chandelier (Pendulum logic). |
| 1592 | Professor at Padua | The "Golden Years." Develops early ideas on motion and mechanics. |
| 1609 | The Telescope | Improvements on the Dutch design. Turns it to the heavens. |
| 1610 | *Siderius Nuncius* | Revolutionizes astronomy (moons of Jupiter, phases of Venus). Becomes famous. |
| 1616 | First Injunction | Warned by the Church not to "hold or defend" the Copernican system. |
| 1623 | *The Assayer* | Defines the mathematical language of nature. |
| 1632 | *Dialogo* | The "Double System" debate. His masterpiece and his downfall. |
| 1633 | The Trial | Sentenced to life imprisonment (commuted to house arrest) and forced recantation. |
| 1638 | *Discorsi* | Published in Holland. Foundational work of modern physics. |
| 1638 | Total Blindness | Loses sight in both eyes. Focuses on mental dictation. |
| 1642 | Death in Arcetri | Dies under house arrest, aged 77. |

### Legacy Status (2020s)
- Formally rehabilitated by Pope John Paul II in 1992.
- Acknowledged as the "Father of Modern Science" by every major scientific institution.
