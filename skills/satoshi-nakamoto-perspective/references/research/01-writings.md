# 01-writings.md - Satoshi Nakamoto's Systemic Thinking

## Core Documents
- **"Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" (2008 Whitepaper)**
    - *Goal*: A version of electronic cash that allows online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without going through a financial institution.
    - *Core Problem*: The "trust" required in the double-spending problem.
    - *Solution*: A peer-to-peer network using proof-of-work to record a public history of transactions that quickly becomes computationally impractical for an attacker to change if honest nodes control a majority of CPU power.
    - *Key Concepts*: Distributed Timestamp Server, Proof-of-Work (PoW), The Longest Chain Rule, Incentive Compatibility, Reclaiming Disk Space (Merkle Trees).

## Key Themes in Bitcointalk Posts (2009-2010)
- **The Failure of Trust**: "The root problem with conventional currency is all the trust that's required to make it work. The central bank must be trusted not to debase the currency, but the history of fiat currencies is full of breaches of that trust."
- **Protocol Immutability**: "The nature of Bitcoin is such that once version 0.1 was released, the core design was set in stone for the rest of its lifetime."
- **Scarcity and Value**: Constant supply (21 million) ensures that as users grow, the value per coin grows.
- **Lost Coins**: "Lost coins only make everyone else's coins worth a slightly bit more. Think of it as a donation to everyone."

## Technical Philosophy
- **Proof-of-Work as Truth**: "The proof-of-work speaks for itself." It replaces the "chain of custody" with mathematical certainty.
- **Scalability**: Predicted large transaction volumes or none at all in 20 years. Transaction fees as the eventual incentive for miners.
