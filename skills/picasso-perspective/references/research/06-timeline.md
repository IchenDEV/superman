# Pablo Picasso · 05-Decisions.md

## Key Turning Points & Strategic Choices

### 1. The Abandonment of Classical Mastery (1906)
- **Context**: He could paint like Raphael by age 14.
- **Decision**: To deliberately de-skill and move toward "ugliness" and "distortion" in *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon*.
- **Logic**: **Destructive Creation.** He realized that "classical perfection" was a dead end. Innovation required the destruction of beauty. [Ref: masterworksfineart.com]

### 2. The Move to Synthetic Cubism (1912)
- **Context**: Analytical Cubism had become so abstract it was "unreadable."
- **Decision**: To introduce "real" objects (collage) back into the work.
- **Logic**: **Synthetic Re-engineering.** Instead of simulating reality, he decided to *build* it from fragments of the world. [Ref: moma.org]

### 3. The *Guernica* Commitment (1937)
- **Context**: The bombing of the Basque town during the Spanish Civil War.
- **Decision**: To use his "private" surrealist language for a "public" political statement.
- **Logic**: **Art as a Weapon.** "Painting is not made to decorate apartments. It is an offensive and defensive weapon of war against the enemy." [Ref: azquotes.com]

### 4. Joining the Communist Party (1944)
- **Context**: The liberation of Paris.
- **Decision**: A public commitment to political ideology.
- **Logic**: **Strategic Branding.** It aligned him with the "people" and provided him with a new "authority" to replace the bourgeois art market he had already conquered.

### 5. The Variations on Velázquez (1957)
- **Context**: His late years.
- **Decision**: To spend months painting 44 variations of *Las Meninas*.
- **Logic**: **Style Cannibalism.** A final, obsessive "trial of strength" with the Old Masters. He wanted to ensure that he didn't just belong to his century, but to all of art history. [Ref: luxe-magazine.com]
