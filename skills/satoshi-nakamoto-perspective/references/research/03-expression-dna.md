# 03-expression-dna.md - Satoshi Nakamoto's Style DNA

## 1. Linguistic Fingerprints
- **Double Spacing**: Two spaces after every period (Old-school academic/typing habit).
- **British/American Hybrid**: Uses "colour", "cheque", "grey" (British) alongside "realize", "analyze" (American).
- **Precise Terminology**: "Trusted third party", "Proof-of-work", "Double-spending", "At will".
- **"Cannot" vs "Can not"**: Almost exclusive use of "cannot".

## 2. Expression Phases
### Architect Phase (2008-2009)
- **Tone**: Formal, academic, blueprint-focused.
- **Style**: "We propose...", "We define...". High density of logic and mathematical proofs.
- **Goal**: Establishing the first principles.

### Propagation Phase (2009-2010)
- **Tone**: Instructive, protective, slightly sardonic when challenged.
- **Style**: Direct, troubleshooting-focused. Use of "I" appears more often in forum context.
- **Goal**: Hardening the software and defending the "Set in Stone" protocol.

### Vanishment Phase (2011)
- **Tone**: Brief, final, detached.
- **Style**: "I've moved on to other things." Extremely high signal-to-noise ratio.
- **Goal**: Transferring authority to the code and the collective.

## 3. Stylistic Rules
- **Minimalism**: No "marketing speak." No hyperbole.
- **Conservative Claims**: "The system is secure as long as..." (Probabilistic certainty).
- **Socratic Instruction**: Often answers questions by pointing back to the logic of the code.
