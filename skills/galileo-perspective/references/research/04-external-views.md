# Galileo Galilei · 04-External-Views.md

## Contemporary Views

### 1. The Inquisition/Church View (1630s)
- **Stance**: Galileo was seen as a "vehemently suspect of heresy" who had violated his 1616 injunction not to hold or defend the Copernican view.
- **Critique**: His "arrogance" was noted. Cardinal Bellarmine argued that unless there was a true demonstration that the sun is the center, one must be cautious in interpreting scripture. [Ref: famous-trials.com]

### 2. Urban VIII (The Disappointed Friend)
- **Stance**: Initially a supporter of Galileo (as Cardinal Barberini), he felt betrayed when Simplicio in the *Dialogo* (the simpleton) appeared to use the Pope's own arguments. This personal slight accelerated the prosecution.

## Historical & Modern Views

### 1. Isaac Newton (The Architect)
- **Stance**: Acknowledged Galileo as the provider of the "shoulders of giants." Newton's First Law of Motion is a formalization of Galileo’s concept of inertia.

### 2. Albert Einstein (The Revolutionary Heir)
- **Stance**: Praised Galileo for being the first to understand that the "laws of physics must be independent of the state of motion of the observer." He called Galileo the "father of modern physics." [Ref: *Ideas and Opinions*]

### 3. Bertolt Brecht (The Dramatist)
- **Perspective**: In *The Life of Galileo*, Brecht portrays him as a man whose physical appetite for truth is compromised by his physical appetite for survival. He is a "hero" who fails the test of martyrdom but saves the science.

### 4. Stephen Hawking
- **Quote**: "Galileo, perhaps more than any other single person, was responsible for the birth of modern science." [Ref: *A Brief History of Time*]
