# Pablo Picasso · 04-External-Views.md

## Contemporary Critiques

### 1. Henri Matisse (The Rival)
- **Stance**: Viewed Picasso’s work as a "boxing match." Initially dismissed *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon* as a "joke" but later recognized its power as a "conceptual" breakthrough. [Ref: artnet.com]

### 2. Gertrude Stein (The Patron)
- **Stance**: Saw him as the "Napoleonic" figure of the art world. She understood that his "ugliness" was the result of a "struggle to be real." [Ref: *The Autobiography of Alice B. Toklas*]

### 3. Louis Vauxcelles (The Namer)
- **Stance**: Dismissed Braque and Picasso’s work as being made of "little cubes," inadvertently naming the movement that would define the century. [Ref: theguardian.com]

## Historical & Psychological Views

### 1. John Richardson (The Biographer)
- **Perspective**: Diagnosed Picasso with "psychic cannibalism." Richardson argued that Picasso’s greatness came from his ability to ingest the "mana" of other artists and periods. [Ref: *A Life of Picasso*]

### 2. Marie-Laure Bernadac (The "Ogre" Theory)
- **Perspective**: Described his process as "acute pictorial cannibalism." Picasso was an "ogre" who could not leave a style alone until he had exhausted it. [Ref: antiquesandthearts.com]

### 3. Feminist Perspectives (The Predatory Critique)
- **Perspective**: Modern critics (and memoirs by Françoise Gilot) highlight the "vampiric" nature of his relationship with women, who served as disposable fuel for his creative transformations. The "Picasso Perspective" is often viewed as inseparable from his "predatory gaze."
