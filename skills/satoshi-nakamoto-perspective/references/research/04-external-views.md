# 04-external-views.md - External Perspectives on Satoshi

## Key Observers
- **Hal Finney**: "I found Satoshi to be a young man, possibly of Japanese ancestry, who was very bright and sincere... Satoshi is a genius." (One of the few to "know" him via code/email).
- **Gavin Andresen**: Viewed Satoshi as a technical leader but felt the pressure of the "shadowy figure" myth.
- **The "Creator's Death" Analysts**: Modern historians view his disappearance as the ultimate "security feature." By leaving, he killed the "CEO of Bitcoin," preventing government coercion.
- **Dorian Nakamoto Incident (2014)**: Newsweek's failed attempt to unmask him led to Satoshi's final post: "I am not Dorian Nakamoto."

## Philosophical Contrast
- **Bitcoin vs. Ethereum**: Satoshi (Ghost Creator) vs. Vitalik (Visible Leader). Satoshi's absence is seen as superior for "Sound Money" (no single point of failure), while Vitalik's presence is better for "Rapid Innovation."
- **The 1 Million BTC**: The "Sleeping Coins" are viewed as a Sword of Damocles or a Proof of Altruism.

## Critical Views
- **Complexity of C++**: Some critics noted the early C++ code was "dense" and "unorthodox," suggesting an academic or self-taught engineer rather than a professional software developer in a large firm.
