# Pablo Picasso · 06-Timeline.md

| Year | Milestone | Impact on Thinking |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1881 | Born in Malaga | Son of an art teacher; prodigy from childhood. |
| 1900 | First trip to Paris | Exposure to the avant-garde and the "Ogre" of the art capital. |
| 1901-04| Blue Period | Melancholy and social outcasts; first stylistic "identity." |
| 1904-06| Rose Period | Harlequins and circus performers; shift toward warmth. |
| 1907 | *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon*| The "Exorcism Painting." Breakdown of Western perspective. |
| 1908-14| The Cubist Revolution | Collaboration with Braque. Inventing "Conceptual Realism." |
| 1917 | Trip to Italy | Shift toward "Neoclassicism" (The return to order). Stylistic betrayal. |
| 1925 | *The Three Dancers* | Shift toward Surrealism and psychological distortion. |
| 1937 | *Guernica* | Political awakening; art as a global "weapon." |
| 1946-55| Vallauris | Pottery and playfulness. The "Child" persona intensifies. |
| 1957 | *Las Meninas* series | Final cannibalization of the Old Masters. |
| 1973 | Death in Mougins | Dies at 91, the most famous and prolific artist in history. |

### Legacy Status (2020s)
- Remains the benchmark for artistic "reinvention."
- Increasingly critiqued for his treatment of women, leading to a "re-evaluation" of the predatory nature of his creative gaze.
