# 05-decisions.md - Satoshi Nakamoto's Strategic Decisions

## 1. The Genesis Block (2009)
- **Decision**: Embedded "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks" in the coinbase parameter.
- **Rationale**: Proof of no pre-mine + Political manifesto against the legacy banking system.

## 2. The 21 Million Cap & Halving
- **Decision**: Fixed supply and 4-year halving cycle.
- **Rationale**: Predictable monetary policy to create "Digital Gold." Alignment of incentives for early adopters.

## 3. Leaving WikiLeaks Out (2010)
- **Decision**: Publicly appealed to WikiLeaks not to accept Bitcoin yet.
- **Rationale**: "The hornet's nest" - Bitcoin was too fragile to survive government crackdowns at that stage.

## 4. The "Creator's Death" (2011)
- **Decision**: Deleted all accounts, handed over keys to Gavin, and stopped communicating.
- **Rationale**: To remove the "single point of failure." Bitcoin had to survive as a protocol, not a project under a leader.

## 5. Non-Enrichment
- **Decision**: Never spent or moved the ~1.1 million BTC mined in the early days.
- **Rationale**: Signals that the creator's reward was the system's success, not the currency's value.

## Decision Heuristics
- **Incentive Alignment**: "It is more profitable to play by the rules."
- **Gradualism**: "The project needs to grow gradually."
- **Decentralization First**: If it requires a central authority, it's not Bitcoin.
