# Pablo Picasso · 02-Conversations.md

## Artistic Rivalries & Collaborations

### 1. The Braque "Rope" (1907-1914)
- **Context**: The invention of Cubism.
- **Dialogue Style**: "Two mountain climbers roped together." They worked in such tight synchronization that they often dressed alike and refused to sign the fronts of their paintings. It was a "conversation of deconstruction." [Ref: artrkl.com]
- **Rupture**: The collaboration ended when Braque went to war. Picasso's "conversation" with Braque was his most intense intellectual partnership.

### 2. The Matisse "Boxing Match"
- **Context**: Rivalry for the leadership of the avant-garde.
- **Tone**: Competitive respect. Matisse was the "North Pole" to Picasso's "South Pole."
- **Insight**: "No one has ever looked at Matisse's painting more carefully than I; and no one has looked at mine more carefully than he." Their works were a decades-long visual debate. [Ref: artnet.com]

## Interviews & Public Statements

### 1. The Zervos Statement (1935)
- **Tone**: Aphoristic, confident, almost mystical.
- **Core Quote**: "A painting is not thought out and settled in advance. While it is being done it changes as one's thoughts change."
- **Insight**: The painting is a living record of the artist's struggle with the object.

### 2. The Gertrude Stein Salon
- **Context**: The intellectual hub of Paris.
- **Dialogue Style**: Picasso was the "bandit" upstart, absorbing the theories of the Steins and Apollinaire. His "conversations" here were a form of intellectual foraging. [Ref: artnet.com]

## The "Ogre" Dynamics
- **Style**: In personal relationships (recorded by Françoise Gilot), his "conversation" was often a tool of psychological domination. He used "visual cannibalism" not just on art, but on the energy of the people around him to fuel his creative engine.
