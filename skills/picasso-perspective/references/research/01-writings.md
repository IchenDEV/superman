# Pablo Picasso · 01-Writings.md

## Core Intellectual Frameworks

### 1. Conceptual Realism (The "Think" over "See")
- **Source:** Statements to Christian Zervos (1935), Interviews with Brassaï.
- **Core Argument:** Painting is not an optical record but a mental inventory. "I paint objects as I think them, not as I see them." [Ref: azquotes.com]
- **Implication:** The artist is not a mirror but a "creator of reality." If you know a face has a back, the back belongs on the canvas alongside the front.

### 2. Multi-Perspective Integration (Mobile Perspective)
- **Source:** *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon* (1907), Analytic Cubism (1908-1912).
- **Core Argument:** Single-point perspective is a lie of the eye. Truth requires a "Total Image" achieved by moving around the object in the mind and collapsing those viewpoints onto a single 2D plane. [Ref: arteidolia.com]

### 3. Destructive Creation (The Phoenix Model)
- **Source:** "Every act of creation is first an act of destruction."
- **Core Argument:** To find the "true" form, one must first dismantle the "obvious" or "pretty" form. Invention requires the courage to sabotage one's own success. [Ref: udlap.mx]

### 4. Synthetic Logic (Collage as Truth)
- **Source:** *Still Life with Chair Caning* (1912).
- **Core Argument:** Instead of painting reality, one can *synthesize* it using fragments of the real world (newspaper, rope, wood grain). This moves the work from "representation" to "object." [Ref: moma.org]

## Self-Created Terms & Concepts
- **"Visual Cannibalism"**: The process of consuming and metabolizing the styles of predecessors and peers. [Ref: antiquesandthearts.com]
- **"Analytical Cubism"**: The geometric deconstruction phase.
- **"Synthetic Cubism"**: The reconstruction and collage phase.
- **"Strength in Reserve"**: Working "below one's means" to maintain artistic tension. [Ref: nvg.org]

## Intellectual Pedigree
- **Ancestors**: **Paul Cézanne** ("the father of us all"), Velázquez (whom he "cannibalized"), African masks (Primitivism). [Ref: masterworksfineart.com]
- **Descendants**: Virtually every modern artist, from Jackson Pollock to David Hockney.
